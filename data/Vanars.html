Confusion Between Monkeys and Vanars

Most of our readers (not all) will be unfamiliar with: the vanars. In Bharat'(Curent day India) vanars were considered half man creatures who played a pivotal role in ramayana. Throughout the ramayana the vanars are described as being similar to humans in their intelligence and other characteristics Most of us will call them monkeys because they have a wiggly tail. Well there's a huge difference between a monkey and vanars. Let's take an example of the most famous and powerful of the vanaras The lord Hanuman the son of the wind - God vayu and was known to display god like powers himself In fact he shows up in another Historical epic the Mahabharata which occurs around 2000years later implying that he is immortal There are different theories about the origin of the myth of vanaras. The word vanara itself is derived from two Sanskrit words: vana {वन(forest)} and Nara{नर(Man} meaning forest man or forest dweller This leads us to the conclusion that the vanaras were a race of men who lived in the forest Perhaps the part about them being a monkey was simple a form of evolution and to justify more we know that When Arjuna meets Hanuman during Mahabharata(Dvapara Yug) in dwapar yugs he asks Hanuman why the bridge of Lanka was not made of wood Lord Hanuman replied that the average warrior of Shri Ram's army was taller stronger and heavier than you (Arjuna) this instance proves that vanars were not monkeys.
